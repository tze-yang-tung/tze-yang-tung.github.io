About Me

My name is Tze-Yang and I am a PhD student at Imperial College London. I specialize in applied machine learning in wireless communication and multi-agent systems. In particular, I'm interested in how machine learning can enable wireless applications that current state-of-the art systems cannot support. For example, error resilient wireless video and wireless VR. I have successfully started a company, called WAIveform, that focuses on this mission. Now I want to bring my skills to industry, looking for roles in applied machine learning research.

Co-founder of WAIveform

WAIVeform is a startup company that focuses on using machine learning to optimize wireless video transmission. Our patented proprietary algorithm simultaneously optimizes video compression and transmission achieving < 1ms video latency and no video dropout whatsoever.

PhD Research Assistant

Imperial College London
Supervisor: Prof. Deniz Gunduz

As a PhD candidate, my research primarily focuses on applied machine learning in wireless communications and multi-agent systems. Specifically, my work focuses on deep learning driven joint source-channel coding and multi-agent collaboration and communications. The greatest impact of my research is how these traditional concepts form the underlying formalism for semantic and goal-oriented communication, driving future 6G telecommunications.
