About Me

My name is Tze-Yang and I am a PhD student at Imperial College London. I specialize in applied machine learning in wireless communication and multi-agent systems. In particular, I'm interested in how machine learning can enable wireless applications that current state-of-the art systems cannot support. For example, ultra-reliable wireless video and wireless VR. I have successfully started a company, called WAIveform, that focuses on this mission. Now I want to bring my skills to industry, looking for roles in applied machine learning research.

Co-founder of WAIveform

WAIVeform is a startup company that focuses on using machine learning to optimize wireless video transmission. Our patented proprietary algorithm simultaneously optimizes video compression and transmission achieving < 1ms video latency and no video dropout whatsoever.

PhD Research Assistant

Imperial College London, IPC-Lab
Supervisor: Prof. Deniz Gunduz

As a PhD candidate, my research primarily focuses on applied machine learning in wireless communications and multi-agent systems. Specifically, my work focuses on deep learning driven joint source-channel coding and multi-agent collaboration and communications. The greatest impact of my research is how these traditional concepts can create the underlying formalism for semantic and goal-oriented communication, driving future 6G telecommunications. My research has won awards, such as the IEEE Prize in Telecommunications.

What I’m good at?

Programming Languages

I have over 7 years of experience in Python and C++, including over 4 years developing machine learning models using Pytorch and Tensorflow. I also have extensive experience using GNURadio with software defined radios.

Hardware

Unlike most machine learning researchers, the models I develop do not simply live in simulation. I have extensive experience deploying machine learning models using C++ and Docker on NVIDIA Jetson devices.

Entrepreneurship

As a PhD student, I have always been fascinated by the interaction between the commercial world and research and how pressing issues in society can be solved by state-of-the-art research. I started my company WAIveform to improve the reliability of wireless video streaming for drones and VR devices. Throughout the startup experience, I not only worked with people across the industry, including Royal College of Art students to understand their challenges when using VR to create, but also entrepreneurs, such as the CEO of Creator Fund, Jamie Macfarlane, who gave me invaluable advice on how to start a company. I also took a course from the Imperial College Business School that taught me the basics of startup entrepreneurship.

System Admin

In the Information Processing and Communications Lab (IPC-Lab), I am the system admin of the group's GPU cluster. I have been the admin for 3 years, growing the cluster from the initial 8 GPUs to now 30+ GPUs. We use a dashboard for monitoring GPU utilization so that everyone in the lab can have access to GPUs, and it is built using PHP and Apache.
